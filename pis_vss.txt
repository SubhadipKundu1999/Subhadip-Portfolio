Basic Components of Touch Screens
• Touch Sensor : Clear Glass panel with touch
responsive surface, touching the screen cause
voltage or signal change.
• Controller : The Interface between touch sensor and
PC, which translate touch into information. Usually
integrated in the display with a USB port.
• Software Driver : Interpret the touch event
information received from controller. Most drivers
are mouse emulation type which makes touch
similar to mouse clicking

Resistive Touch Screen
❖ A resistive touch screen works by detecting physical pressure to close an
open electrical circuit formed by two separate, flexible layers
❖ This type of touch screen has two layers separated by spacers,
electrical current runs through these two layers
❖ Glass/acrylic panel is coated with electrically conductive and
resistive layer separated by tiny separator dots
❖It is pressure sensitive and responds to any input device,
including finger, gloved hand or pen stylus.
❖It is a reliable and affordable technology that is widely used
❖When touched, the two layers make contact which changes the
electrical field and coordinates of the touch are calculated based
on the disruption of the electrical field

25 Working of SAW screen
Surface Acoustic Wave touch screen
• Two transducers placed on the x-axis (Trans) and y-axis (Recv.) that
constantly emit ultrasonic sound waves with Piezoelectric Transducers.
• Does not require conductive coating like resistive and capacitive
• Reflectors are placed opposite to the transducers to reflect those signals
• An interruption in the sound waves due to touch, causes a change in the
amplitude of those waves. The controller and driver can then calculate
where that occurred.
• SAW screen is a good choice for applications where image clarity is
important, viz. KIOSKs, Ticket Vending Machine
• Do not perform well in extremely dusty environments, susceptible to
false touch or failure if contaminated with dirt, water ,grease etc.
• Responds to finger, gloved hand or soft stylus which absorbs ultrasonic
waves.

▪ When a user touches the screen, with finger or stylus interrupts
the infrared light beams
▪ The system then calculates the coordinates of touch point based
on the specific beams that were interrupted. This information
processed and interprets the touch input and performs the
corresponding action.

RDSO/SPN/TC/108/2019 V er. 1.0 Key Points
System Architecture & Networking
❖ Core Infrastructure: The system is built on a standard IPv4 Ethernet
network using OFC or CAT-6 cables.
❖ Central Data Controller (CDC): Features two industrial-grade CPUs in
redundant mode for continuous data synchronization. Minimum
hardware: i5 processor, 4 GB RAM, and 500 GB SSD.
❖ Platform Data Controller (PDC): Acts as a gateway for each platform. It
routes data from the CDC to display boards (TADDB/CGDB) and monitors
device health.
❖ Connectivity: Uses Layer 3 Central Data Switches (CDS). Communication
relies on TCP/IP (Port 25000) for point-to-point data and UDP (Port
25-May-26 26000) for broadcast commands like "Start" or "Stop." 77
RDSO/SPN/TC/108/2019 V er. 1.0 Key Points
Display Board Categories
• True Colour Video Displays (OVD & IVD): Outdoor and indoor video walls used
for train information, commercial advertisements, and promotional videos.
Supports 6, 12, or 18 lines.
•Monochrome Multiline Display (MLDB): Placed at station entrances to show
multiple trains (max 10 lines).
•Platform Display Board (PFD): Shows information for a single train specific to
that platform.
•At-A-Glance Board (AGDB): Alternates between train information and full coach
composition.
•Coach Guidance Display (CGDB): Double-faced boards showing Coach No. and
Train No. alternatively to guide passengers on the platform. 25-May-26 78

History of CCTV over Indian Railway
25-May-26
197
1. Early to Mid-2000s: Initial installations were isolated, covering only high-security or critical zones
2. 2014: The Railway Board formally issued instructions to Zonal Railways to extend CCTV usage
beyond security, utilizing camera networks to monitor cleanliness and passenger amenities
at major stations.
3. 2017–2020: The government launched a massive, structured rollout to install IP-based Video
Surveillance Systems (VSS) across hundreds of stations. This focused on covering waiting
halls, foot-over-bridges, and platforms, and included facial recognition and AI tracking to
bolster security.
4. 2023: Onboard surveillance became a major priority. By late 2023, the system had been
expanded to cover nearly 12,000 train coaches, targeting crime-prone stretches and
securing vestibules and entryways.
5. Mid-2025: The Ministry of Railways approved a massive nationwide rollout to install CCTV
systems across all 74,000 passenger coaches and 15,000 locomotives.

Open Network Video Interface Forum(ONVIF)
❖ A global standard that allows different brands of IP security devices to
work together seamlessly
❖ It allows an open-platform camera to talk directly to a different brand's
recorder without software compatibility issues
❖ Customers can mix and match hardware brands based on cost and
features rather than being forced to buy from a single manufacturer
❖ Legacy systems can easily integrate with newly launched cameras or
software as long as both support the same ONVIF profile
❖ ONVIF uses letter-coded "Profiles" to group specific features together.
Devices must share the same profile to communicate
25-May-26
243
ONVIF Profiles
❖ Profile S: The basic standard for IP video streaming, audio streaming,
and pan-tilt-zoom (PTZ) controls.
❖ Profile G: Designed for edge storage and retrieval, governing how
video is recorded and played back from onboard SD cards.
❖ Profile T: The modern standard for advanced video streaming,
supporting high-efficiency video compression (H.265) and analytics.
❖ Profile M: Metadata configuration for AI applications, allowing
devices to communicate analytics like facial recognition or object
classification
25-May-26
244
ONVIF Profile Compatibility for Cameras
• Supported Profiles (Video & Analytics): A modern IP camera typically
supports Profile S (basic video streaming), Profile T (advanced streaming
and H.265 compression), and Profile M (AI metadata and analytics data).
• Edge Storage Profiles: If the camera has a built-in microSD card slot for
local recording, it will also support Profile G (edge storage and retrieval).
• Unsupported Profiles (Access Control): A standard security camera will
never support Profile C (door control) or Profile A (access control
configuration), as these are strictly meant for physical access control panels
and card readers.

Power over Ethernet (PoE) is technology that passes electric power over
twisted-pair Ethernet cable to powered devices (PD), such as wireless
access points, IP cameras, and VoIP phones in addition to the data that
cable usually carries. It enables one RJ45 cable to provide both data
connection and electric power to PDs instead of having a separate cable for
each devices.
Power over Ethernet (PoE)
A standard PoE system requires two types of devices:
1. Power Sourcing Equipment (PSE): The device that injects power into the cable.
This is usually a PoE Network Switch or a PoE NVR (Network Video Recorder).
2. Powered Device (PD): The device receiving the power, such as your IP security
camera

Powered Device (PD)
Network device that is powered by PoE is referred to as a powered device, or PD. Wireless
access points, IP security cameras and VoIP phones are common examples. The rise of the
more powerful IEEE 802.3bt standard has paved the way for more power-hungry HighSpeed HD Outdoor PoE network cameras.
Power Sourcing Equipment (PSE)
PSE devices send power and data over the Ethernet cable to a connected PD. PSE devices
are classified as either “midspan” or “end-span.”

PoE budget , maximum power per port , average power per port
❑ A PoE budget — also known as a power budget — refers to the total power in watts
that a PoE switch or injector (PSE) can provide across all its output ports. PoE
budgets vary widely across PSE devices, but all budgets directly relate to how much
power per port is available to devices. Knowing the PoE budget is crucial because an
inadequate power budget can cause devices not to work.
❑ In addition to the PoE budget, the spec sheets of many PoE switches and injectors
state the maximum output power per port. This number can be an indicator of the
highest PoE standard the switch complies with.
❑ For example, a switch with a max. output power per port up to 30 W is a PoE+
switch; an injector with an output up to 60 W per port is a PoE++ injector or an Ultra
PoE injector.

ENDSPAN
Also called endpoint, the typical end-span is a PoE Network Switch. Since the
switch itself can power connected devices, there is no need for an additional
power source between the PoE switch (PSE) and the connected PoE edge
device (PD).

MIDSPAN
If a non-PoE-enabled network switch is to be used with a PoE device, an
power source that adds power to the connection is required. This device
is placed between ("in the middle") the non-PoE network switch and the
PoE device. A very common type of a PoE midspan is a PoE injector.

Advantages of PoE
1. The delivery of data and power over a standard Ethernet cable eliminates the need
for AC/DC power supplies and outlets to give power to PD equipment. That lowers
the costs of adding or installing compatible PD equipment.
2. With PoE technology there are fewer points of failure. By connecting PoE switch or
PoE injector to an uninterruptible power supply (UPS) and PoE powered devices are
guaranteed a constant power delivery, which is important for mission-critical PoE
edge devices.
3. With PoE installations, which utilize managed PoE switches, offer the ability to
remotely access PoE devices. This function helps to greatly reduce the downtime of
your connected devices.

CCD Sensor
• CCD sensors are an integrated grid of semiconductor capacitors
capable of holding an electrical charge.
• When light reaches the sensor, these capacitors, acting as
photosites, absorb the light and convert it into an electrical charge.
• The amount of charge at each photosite is directly proportional to
the intensity of the light that strikes it.
• Charge from each photosite is transferred through the sensor's
grid (hence the term charge-coupled) and read at one corner of
the array
• This method ensures a high degree of image quality and
uniformity because each pixel uses the same pathway to output its
signal
